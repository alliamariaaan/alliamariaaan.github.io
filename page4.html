THE REASON BEHIND

by Allia Marian Cruz

        Aubrey and I are both from a town na palaging baha, yung seryosong baha talaga. ‘Yung tipong maulan lang baha na, we are used to it so we are not that complaining about it but we know that this crisis should end. I am from Obando while Aubrey’s from Calumpit, kaya alam na agad naming kung anong topic talaga ang dapat naming pagtuunan ng pansin, ang isyu sa pagbaha. Then we both decided to focus on waste segregation because we know that this is one of the main reasons in severe flooding on our area. We started to compare the both town, to see what’s worth to be in our website and needs more improvement in this issue, and in the end we chose our little town, Obando.

        Obando, Bulacan is known for its annual Fertility Dance Festival every May 17 – 19. Every year, it is always extravagant, almost half a million people attend to dance to ask for a child or simply giving thanks to the blessings they have received. This small town is composed of only 11 barangays; three of them are actually an island who are somewhat near Malolos, and Hagonoy. The north side is the town of Bulakan, south is Malabon City, east side are Valenzuela, and Meycauayan, and west side of Obando is the vast Manila Bay, that’s why it was always flooding.


        And the thing that I hate about Obando, kahit na taga roon ako ay palaging amoy basura, at sobrang malangaw. That is the downside for not using the Obando Landfill, they built it but they didn’t use because it is in the middle of the Manila Bay. The people are angry to the former mayor for building it, they think that this will destroy the waters of Obando, and the mere fact that one of our patron saints is Nuestra Senora de Salambao, so the dumping of garbages to the new landfill didn’t pushed through and they settled to dump it in a mini landfill in the end of Obando. It is just beside the main road so you will see the trashes clearly while traveling. The city had no proper garbage disposal so that the people just throw everything in any trashcans.


    Proper waste disposal or waste segregation is the collection, processing, and recycling or deposition of the waste materials of human of human society. The term “waste” covers both solid waste (refuse, or garbage) and sewage (wastewater). And I think waste segregation is one of the answers to reduce and towards the cleaner and odor-free Obando. It might be a hassle to have more than three types of garbage cans in each house but it is for the better.
This website’s focus is to inform not only the people of Obando but all of the humans who are willing to help our Mother Earth to get better. We are both happy to see you towards the greener environment, waste segregation is just the first step to this campaign and I know that you can do more, tiwala lang. Fighting!
